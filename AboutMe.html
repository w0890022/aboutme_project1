About Me

My Hometown


I grew up in Wisconsin. My family is from West Allis, WI. My parents raised me away from the big city. I grew up in Grand Marsh, WI. It's about a 20-minute drive to Wisconsin Dells. It's home to the largest waterparks. Though, honestly, I doubt it's actually that large. I think something like Disney or Six Flags is probably larger. Where I feel at home is always going to be the greater Milwaukee area. Most of my family lives in the area.


My parents went to Hamilton High in Milwaukee, WI. They met after high school and married in May 1989. I was born June 1990, we lived in Mikwaukee, WI until I was a year old. When I think of places that I loved being, it was grandma's house. She lived in Muskego, WI in a cul de sac off of Racine Avenue. Wisconsin is home to more than 15,000 lakes. We spent most of our summers enjoying swimming in some local lakes. We went fishing every year, and my dad enjoyed ice fishing with his old buddy Jack. I remember going ice fishing once, that was my last time. We had spent quite a few summer vacations to Eagle River, WI. We would stay on the chain of lakes. When I picture home, I can see the corn fields, pine trees, the lakes. The friday night fish fry. Where you can almost tell every neighbor is grilling out all summer long. My parent's adapted to winters and cooked in the garage even. We have some of the best cheese. It's something I get every time I visit family. I also miss being around the rugged hills. The natural beauty of Wisconsin, it's not about the beer we have, or what lake is the best spot for fishing, it's the culture we've made.


Education


I went to Adams Friendship High School. Most of the classes I did enjoy were my electives in desktop publishing, keyboarding, accounting, French, and webpage design. I went to school from 2004 to 2007. I graduated earlier than my class. I didn't have a great experience in school; if someone asked me, I barely got by. My school didn't help me or have an IEP like they do today. I was never tested for ADD or APD. I had originally planned to take computer programming, ceramics and another year of french class in my senior year.


If I had the help I needed back then, I probably would have gone to college sooner. I spent 16 years undiagnosed without being able to focus on a future. When I moved to Windsor, I was able to get diagnosed and finally knew I wanted to go back to school. In 2025, I narrowed down my list of places I wanted to go to college and applied for St. Clair College in Computer Programming. I took ACE distance in communications before classes began in 2026.


My Family


My main family lives in Wisconsin. I have a brother who is having his second little girl at the end of May. They just moved into a new house in Sussex, WI. My first niece is Hannah. She will be 2 years old on June 30. My mom remarried after my dad passed away. They live in Waukesha, WI. Her wedding anniversary to my stepdad is coming up the beginning of March. They go to Laughlin, NV, every year where they got married. My dad passed away a few days after my 20th birthday in 2010. He was 45 years old. I can still remember him telling me as a child he wouldn't live forever. He knew his time was limited. I just never thought it would be before he got to see me get married or see his first granddaughter Hannah.


All of my grandparents are gone already. On my mom's side, we lost my grandpa, Casper, but everyone called him Cap, in 1998. I don't really remember him much other than that he was Italian and he didn't like his picture taken. My grandma, she was my favorite, Audrey. I spent every summer with her for two weeks, sometimes a bit longer. My cousins lived next door, so it was always full of events. We lost her on Halloween in 2013 to pancreatic cancer.


My mom has 2 siblings, Aunt Gina and Uncle Tom. They had a falling out with Uncle Tom when Grandma passed, so holidays haven't been the same since. I have a cousin, Anna, from Aunt Gina and Uncle Pete; she graduates college in Minnesota in June 2027. She's the only child and my youngest first cousin from my moms side. They live in Milwaukee, WI. They've been in the same house since 2001. Anna was born in November 2004. My cousins from Uncle Tom and Aunt Mary are all out of college. They are the one's who lived in a side by side next to grandma after grandpa had passed. Monica became a massage therapist, who married first to Charlie in 2020, and they have 2 beautiful children now, Charles Thomas and Mable Grace. Laura is 35 years old; she went to culinary school. I went to her wedding in 2019 to Brad, and they now have a little boy, Vinny. Jenna is the youngest; she's a vet tech. She has a wild side that she doesn't get from Uncle Tom. She was born on my mom's birthday. I can remember holding her when she was born in 2000. She was the first baby I ever held. They all live close to Uncle Tom in Waterford, WI.


My dad's side isn't as exciting. I won't be mentioning my grandfather, but I am happy he passed away in 2019. My grandma Maryann was a strange woman. She could see a rummage sale sign a mile away. Needless to say, all our Christmas and birthday gifts were always from sales she found. She cooked alot of german food, I didn't like. I never really wanted to spend alot of time with her. She used to have a genuine draft in her hand before lunch. If she saw a flower on the side of the road she really liked, she had a small shovel and extra pots in her trunk even. One time, she saw some nice rocks by our home she had to grab them for her garden back in Mikwaukee. Grandma Maryann was born in 1945 and passed away from cancer in 2011. She had 5 children.


My dad was the oldest born in 1965; Aunt Christine in 1968, Uncle Mike in 1970, and then grandma did remarry and have Uncle Kevin in 1978; and Aunt Gina in 1979. Uncle Mike, from what I heard, overdosed, but he wasn't right after his divorce, probably in 1999, the ex took his boys from him. This was before he ever went on drugs. His boys are Gavin and Grant. Aunt Christine liked some guy who was in prison; she had two boys. Dominique and Everett, I haven't seen them in years, but I know they look like they're happy adults now; Dominique even got married. However, with the grandfather issue, she became his power of attorney and took him for everything he had. Things that should have been split between the siblings; instead, she lived a very rich life.


The younger aunt and uncle from the second marriage, I wouldn't say I'm even related to. They've been in and out of prison for theft, coke, heroin, meth, vandalism, battery, and assault. Hopefully Uncle Kevin never had any children running around. Aunt Gina did have 3 girls, all who got taken away and adopted several years ago. I recently found my oldest cousin Gianna. She's living in Hawaii and currently going to college in the medical field. I don't know her younger siblings very well, she was born in 2002. My dad adored her. She's told me her younger sisters all got to stay together. The younger ones, Lana and Brianna, are both still with their adopted family in Wisconsin. Lana was born in 2008, so she was about two the last time I saw them. Brianna was born in 2012 and I never met her.
